Resilient Tactical Networks for Contested Battlespaces

Tactical communications infrastructure provides the connectivity backbone for multi-domain operations, ensuring warfighters maintain command and control in GPS-denied and electronically contested environments. From resilient mesh networks to anti-jamming waveforms, we engineer communications systems that operate when adversaries attempt to disrupt or intercept. Our platforms integrate with existing defense networks while delivering next-generation capabilities for JADC2 and beyond.

Battle-hardened communications maintaining connectivity when adversaries attempt disruption.

Key Capabilities

Resilient Tactical Mesh Networks

Self-healing mobile ad-hoc networks with adaptive routing maintain secure communications in GPS-denied, jammed, or disconnected battlespace environments with automatic path reconfiguration.

Anti-Jamming Waveforms

Frequency-hopping spread spectrum and directional antennas defeat hostile electronic warfare, maintaining reliable communications despite sophisticated jamming attempts across contested spectrum.

Software-Defined Radios (SDR)

Reconfigurable waveforms and adaptive modulation schemes enable rapid response to changing operational requirements and electromagnetic environments without hardware replacement.

Satellite Communications (SATCOM)

Resilient beyond-line-of-sight connectivity through military SATCOM terminals with anti-jam capabilities, ensuring global reach for expeditionary operations and strategic command.

Low Probability of Intercept (LPI)

Covert communications using ultra-wideband and adaptive power control prevent adversary detection and direction-finding, critical for special operations and intelligence collection.

Cross-Domain Gateway Integration

Secure information exchange between tactical edge and strategic networks across multiple classification levels with automated data sanitization and access control.
