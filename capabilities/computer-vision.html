AI-Powered Visual Intelligence for ISR and Autonomous Systems

Computer vision transforms raw sensor data into actionable intelligence, enabling autonomous systems to perceive and understand complex battlespace environments. From multi-spectral target recognition to real-time threat detection, our algorithms process petabytes of imagery with human-level accuracy at machine speed. Sensor fusion integrates disparate data streams—visible light, infrared, radar, LiDAR—into unified situational awareness that gives warfighters decisive advantage.

Deep learning vision systems extracting intelligence from multi-spectral imagery at scale.

Key Capabilities

Multi-Spectral Object Detection

Deep learning models detect and classify targets across visible, infrared, and SAR imagery with >95% accuracy, operating in degraded visual environments including night, fog, and smoke.

Real-Time Threat Recognition

Edge-deployed neural networks identify weapons, vehicles, and hostile activity in milliseconds, enabling automated alerts and autonomous defensive responses before human reaction time.

Multi-Sensor Fusion Pipelines

Kalman filtering and probabilistic data association fuse camera, LiDAR, radar, and infrared streams into unified environmental models with uncertainty quantification for autonomous navigation.

Automated ISR Analysis

Computer vision pipelines process terabytes of drone and satellite imagery automatically, flagging anomalies and extracting intelligence at scales impossible for human analysts.

3D Scene Reconstruction

Structure-from-motion and SLAM algorithms generate high-fidelity 3D models of terrain and structures from multi-view imagery, enabling mission planning and autonomous navigation.

Activity & Behavior Analysis

Temporal models track movement patterns and detect anomalous behaviors in surveillance feeds, identifying threats through deviations from baseline activity profiles.
